
    Question: How many ways can we arrange 4 distinct mathematics books, 6 distinct physics books, and 3 distinct chemistry books on a shelf if they must be grouped by subject? 
    Answer Choices:
    a) 4!+6!+3!=38
    b) 3!+4!+6!=30
    c) 3!4!6!3=432
    d) 4!×6!×3!=1728
    Solution: The correct answer is c) 3!4!6!3=432. There are 3! ways to choose the order of the subjects, and for each subject, there are different ways to arrange the books, leading to a total of 3!4!6!3=432 possible arrangements.
